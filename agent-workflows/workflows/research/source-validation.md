# Source Validation Workflow

Process for verifying the reliability of sources before using them in research or decision-making.

## Steps

### 1. Identify Source Type

- Primary source: original data, official documentation, research paper.
- Secondary source: summary, analysis, news article.
- Tertiary source: aggregator, wiki, forum post.

### 2. Evaluate Credibility

- **Authority**: Is the author or organization qualified on this topic?
- **Currency**: When was it published? Is the information still valid?
- **Accuracy**: Are claims supported by evidence or citations?
- **Bias**: Does the source have a commercial, political, or ideological motivation?

### 3. Cross-Reference

- Verify key claims against at least one independent source.
- If only one source exists, flag the claim as unverified.
- Note contradictions between sources explicitly.

### 4. Grade the Source

| Grade | Meaning | Use |
|-------|---------|-----|
| A | Primary, peer-reviewed, current | Cite directly |
| B | Reputable secondary, well-sourced | Cite with attribution |
| C | Unverified, single-source, or dated | Use with caveat |
| D | Biased, unsourced, or contradicted | Do not use without corroboration |

### 5. Record

- Include source grade when citing in research outputs.
- Keep a source log for ongoing research projects.
